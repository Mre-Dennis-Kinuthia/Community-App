**Prepared for:** Impact Hub Nairobi Management Team  
**Document type:** Strategic concept note (non-technical)

**Status:** For review and discussion

## 1. Executive Summary

Impact Hub Nairobi has grown into one of the city’s most active ecosystems for social entrepreneurs, innovators, and changemakers. Today, member engagement spans in-person programming, coworking, partnerships, events, and curated opportunities — but much of this value is delivered through fragmented channels: spreadsheets, email threads, WhatsApp groups, manual booking processes, and disconnected partner lists.

The **Impact Hub Nairobi Community Platform** is a purpose-built digital home for the hub — a single place where members discover, connect, book, learn, and grow, and where staff run day-to-day operations with clarity and control.

This is not a generic coworking app. It is an **impact ecosystem platform** designed around how Impact Hub Nairobi actually works: free community access at the entry level, a premium founder tier (Star Connect), bespoke institutional partnerships, curated programs and opportunities, and a member directory that turns strangers into collaborators.

**The opportunity:** Move from managing a community *around* tools to operating a community *through* one integrated platform — strengthening member retention, revenue visibility, partnership value, and Impact Hub’s position as Nairobi’s leading impact entrepreneurship hub.

## 2. Background & Context

### About Impact Hub Nairobi

Impact Hub Nairobi is part of the global Impact Hub network — more than 100 hubs across 60+ countries, supporting over 24,000 entrepreneurs worldwide. Locally, the hub was established in 2022 and has built a reputation around:

- **Programs and acceleration** for ventures moving from idea to scale  
- **Business development advisory** in strategy, fundraising, and investor readiness  
- **Events and community** that connect founders, creatives, investors, and policy shapers  
- **Coworking and workspace access**, delivered in partnership with Ikigai (Westlands, Nairobi)  
- **Consulting, research, and ecosystem development** for institutions and partners  

The hub’s mission remains clear: **build a just and sustainable society** by helping ventures scale measurable social impact — advancing people, planet, and profit through locally rooted solutions.

### The Ecosystem Today

Impact Hub Nairobi serves a diverse community:

| Audience | Who they are |
|----------|--------------|
| **Community members** | Entrepreneurs, creatives, professionals, and students passionate about positive change |
| **Star Connect founders** | Early- and growth-stage ventures ready for dedicated support and global network access |
| **Organisational partners** | NGOs, government, corporates, and institutions seeking co-designed ecosystem engagement |
| **Ecosystem partners** | Investors, funders, workspace partners, and programme collaborators surfaced to members |

Impact themes cut across agriculture, climate, circularity, e-mobility, digitization, gender equity, health, education, finance, and the creative economy.

### Why Now

As the hub scales programming, membership, and partnerships, three pressures are converging:

1. **Member expectations** — Founders and partners expect seamless digital experiences: one login, clear event registration, easy workspace booking, and discoverable opportunities.  
2. **Operational load** — Staff spend significant time on manual coordination: booking confirmations, membership follow-ups, event guest lists, opportunity curation, and payment tracking.  
3. **Impact visibility** — Funders, partners, and the global Impact Hub network increasingly ask for evidence of engagement, reach, and outcomes — not anecdotes.

A dedicated community platform addresses all three without changing what Impact Hub Nairobi stands for. It amplifies it.

## 3. The Challenge

### For Members

Today, members may need to:

- Register for events through one channel, book workspace through another, and discover opportunities through social media or email  
- Reach out manually to find collaborators, mentors, or partners in the community  
- Navigate unclear membership tiers and payment processes  
- Miss opportunities because information arrives late or in the wrong place  

**Result:** Friction reduces participation. Value is harder to see. The community feels less connected than it actually is.

### For Staff

Hub teams currently juggle:

- Event creation, registration tracking, and check-in across separate tools  
- Workspace availability, pricing, and booking status without a single operational view  
- Star Connect applications and organisational partnership inquiries via email  
- News, resources, and partner listings updated in multiple places  
- Limited real-time visibility into membership growth, revenue, and engagement  

**Result:** More admin, less time for programming, partnerships, and member support.

### For Leadership

Without integrated data, it is difficult to answer strategic questions confidently:

- How many active members do we have, and how engaged are they?  
- Which programs, events, and spaces drive the most value?  
- Are Star Connect and organisational memberships converting and renewing?  
- What is our monthly booking and membership revenue?  
- How effectively are we connecting members to partners and opportunities?  

**Result:** Decisions rely on partial information. Reporting to partners and the global network takes extra effort.

## 4. Proposed Solution

The Impact Hub Nairobi Community Platform is a **two-part system** built for the hub’s specific model:

### A. Member Platform (Community App)

The public-facing home for everyone in the Impact Hub Nairobi ecosystem. Members use it to:

- Join the community and complete their profile  
- Browse and connect with other members  
- Register for events and access tickets  
- Book hot desks, meeting rooms, and inquire about private offices and event spaces  
- Discover curated programs, grants, fellowships, and jobs  
- Access a resource library of tools and guides  
- Explore the partner directory and ecosystem opportunities  
- Read hub news and receive notifications  
- Manage membership, billing, and payments (including M-Pesa)  

It is designed as the **digital front door** — the place members return to between in-person visits, events, and programming.

### B. Staff Operations Portal (Admin App)

A separate, role-based portal for Impact Hub staff. It gives teams the tools to:

- Publish news, events, resources, and opportunities  
- Manage the member directory and membership tiers  
- Configure workspaces, pricing, and amenities  
- Monitor and manage bookings and attendance  
- Process Star Connect applications and organisational registrations  
- Run billing, subscriptions, payment links, and financial reporting  
- Maintain the partner directory  
- Handle support tickets and send member notifications  
- View platform health through dashboards and attention queues  

Staff specialists (content, community, programs, finance) each see the modules relevant to their role. Leadership retains full oversight through a command-centre view.

**Both parts work from the same live information** — when staff publish an event or approve a membership, members see it immediately. There is no duplicate data entry across systems.

## 5. What Members Experience

### Joining the Community

A prospective member visits the platform, creates a free account, and completes a short onboarding profile: who they are, what sector they work in, their goals, and how they want to engage. Within minutes, they land on a personalised dashboard with upcoming events, booking shortcuts, community highlights, and guided next steps.

There is no paywall at entry. **Community membership is free** — lowering barriers and widening the funnel for Nairobi’s impact ecosystem.

### Three Membership Pathways

| Tier | Who it’s for | What they get |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| **Community** (Free) | Anyone passionate about impact | Member events, coworking access, community socials, newsletter, community channels |
| **Star Connect** (KES 13,000/month) | Founders ready to scale | Global Passport (120+ hubs), business development support, acceleration programs, grants and funding linkages, strategic advisory |
| **Organisational** (Custom) | Institutions and partners | Bespoke co-designed engagement, program and event co-creation, network access, dedicated partnership manager |

Each pathway has a clear journey on the platform — from registration to application to billing — with human follow-up where it matters (Star Connect review, organisational partnership design).

### Daily Member Activities

**Connect:** Search the member directory by industry, skills, role, and location. View rich profiles. Send connection requests. Discover mutual connections, shared events, and collaboration potential.

**Learn & participate:** Browse upcoming and past events. Register online (free or paid). Receive tickets and reminders. Access workshops, networking sessions, and hub programming in one calendar.

**Work:** Book a hot desk or meeting room at Ikigai-partnered workspace locations. See real-time availability, pricing, and membership benefits. Pay via M-Pesa or card. Manage upcoming and past bookings.

**Grow:** Explore curated opportunities — grants, fellowships, accelerators, and roles — scouted for Impact Hub members. Download resources from the hub library. Connect with ecosystem partners offering funding and support.

**Stay informed:** Read hub news and announcements. Receive in-app notifications when new opportunities or events are published.

## 6. What Staff Experience

The admin portal is organised around how Impact Hub teams actually work:

### Content & Programming Team
- Create and publish events, news, and resources  
- Manage event registrations and check in guests on the day  
- Schedule content for future publication  

### Community Team
- View and support the member directory  
- Manage workspace listings (rooms, desks, pricing, photos, house rules)  
- Monitor bookings and space utilisation  
- Curate the partner directory  

### Programs Team
- Add and publish scouted opportunities (funding, fellowships, roles)  
- Set status (draft, open, closed) and feature priority programs  
- Notify members when new opportunities go live  

### Finance & Operations
- Monitor payments, invoices, subscriptions, and failed transactions  
- Send membership payment links and track renewals  
- Review booking revenue and accounts receivable  
- Access audit logs for accountability  

### Leadership (Command Centre)
- See platform snapshot: member growth, event activity, open support tickets, pending payments, revenue  
- Identify what needs attention today  
- Track engagement trends over time  

Each role sees only what they need — reducing risk and keeping operations focused.

| Hub service | Platform capability |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Programs & acceleration | Opportunities hub, event programming, Star Connect application flow |
| Business development | Member profiles, partner directory, founder tier management |

| Co-working & spaces | Workspace booking, pricing, availability, payment |

The platform also reflects **Kenyan market realities**: pricing in KES, M-Pesa as a primary payment method, Nairobi-centric workspace locations, and impact themes relevant to local ventures.

And it connects to the **global Impact Hub network** — particularly through Star Connect’s Global Passport, giving Nairobi founders access to 120+ hubs worldwide while keeping local community at the centre.

## 9. How It Changes Day-to-Day Operations

### Before → After (Examples)

| Scenario | Today (typical) | With the platform |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Member wants to book a meeting room | Email or walk-in; manual confirmation | Self-service booking with instant confirmation and payment |
| Founder applies for Star Connect | Form or email; tracked in inbox | Structured application; staff review in support queue; approval triggers billing |
| Hub publishes a new grant opportunity | Social post + newsletter (maybe) | Published to Opportunities; all members notified in-app |
| Event day check-in | Paper list or spreadsheet | Digital guest list with one-click check-in |
| Leadership asks “how many members joined this month?” | Manual count or estimate | Dashboard shows 7-day and 30-day signups, verified accounts, profile completeness |
| Partner wants to reach Impact Hub founders | Ad hoc introductions | Partner profile with listed opportunities; members discover and express interest |

These shifts do not replace the human relationships that define Impact Hub. They **protect staff time for those relationships** by removing repetitive coordination.

## 10. Membership & Revenue Model

The platform supports Impact Hub Nairobi’s existing tier structure without forcing change:

**Community (Free)** — Drives top-of-funnel growth. Members register, engage with events, and experience the hub before upgrading. No payment friction at signup.

**Star Connect (Paid)** — Application-based with staff approval. Upon acceptance, members manage subscription and billing on-platform. Revenue is tracked alongside booking income.

**Organisational (Custom)** — Registration captures institutional intent; partnerships team receives structured notification and follows up to co-design engagement. Payment arrangements managed through the finance module.

**Workspace bookings** — Additional revenue stream with member-aware pricing (membership benefits applied automatically where configured).

This creates a **clear monetisation picture**: free community builds reach; Star Connect and organisational tiers build depth; workspace bookings build utilisation revenue.

## 12. Success Metrics

Impact Hub Nairobi management can measure platform value through clear, non-technical indicators:

### Community Health
- Total registered members and monthly new signups  
- Profile completion rate  
- Connection requests sent and accepted  
- Event registration and attendance rates  

### Operational Efficiency
- Average time to respond to Star Connect applications  
- Percentage of bookings completed self-service (vs. manual)  
- Support ticket resolution time  
- Staff hours saved on manual coordination (qualitative + time tracking)  

### Financial Performance
- Monthly booking revenue (KES)  
- Active Star Connect subscriptions and renewal rate  
- Organisational partnership conversions  
- Payment success rate (M-Pesa and card)  

### Ecosystem Impact
- Opportunities published and member views/applications  
- Partner directory engagement  
- Resource library downloads  
- Member-reported value (periodic survey)  

These metrics feed directly into reporting for board updates, partner reviews, and global Impact Hub network submissions.

## 13. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Low member adoption | Free tier removes barrier; staff promote at events; onboarding guides first actions |
| Staff capacity to manage platform | Role-based admin access; start with core modules; phased feature activation |
| Duplication with existing tools | Platform replaces fragmented tools gradually; single source of truth for member data |
| Member privacy concerns | Clear privacy policy; members control profile visibility; staff access is role-gated and audited |
| Payment or booking errors | Structured confirmation flows; finance ops dashboard for failed payments; manual override when needed |
